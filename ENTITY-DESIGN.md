## 1. Core Mission

> Provide for **physical and meaning needs** in a disrupted AI labor society.
> Not waiting for government, corporations, or UBI to do this.
> Building a **global economic parallel system**.

The Collectiv is a Public Benefit Corporation (PBC) / C Corp, eventually held by a perpetual trust. Its purpose is to become a permanent, privately-held, worker-governed, capital-absorbing vehicle — something that doesn't yet exist in pure form — with a **250-year horizon**.

**Key identity**: "Which country you're from or which company you work for becomes less relevant than if you are a member of The Collectiv."

## 2. Entity Architecture (Multi-Layer)

### Phase 1 → Phase 2 Evolution

```
Phase 1: US PBC / C Corp (high-growth startup mode)
Phase 2: Perpetual Trust above the PBC
         ├── Swiss Data Entity (Cloud Act protected, all member data)
         ├── US PBC (operating parent)
         │    ├── Individual LLCs (members' capital accounts)
         │    ├── Operating LLCs (business assets)
         │    └── ...
         ├── EU Entity (data stays in EU)
         ├── Asia Entity
         └── ...
```

### Swiss Data Sovereignty
- The perpetual trust must own the Swiss entity **directly** (not through the US PBC)
- The Swiss entity licenses data processing rights to the operating PBC
- No US court can compel a Swiss entity owned by a Swiss trust structure
- Likely vehicle: Swiss **Stiftung** (foundation) — no shareholders, no members, just a purpose and a board

### Risk Isolation
- Each operating business is its own LLC
- Each country has its own entity
- Liabilities of one can't reach the parent or the trust
- Capital moves between entities tax-efficiently (Netherlands/Switzerland holding companies in the stack)

## 3. Governance Structure

### Three Branches (Foundation Level)

| Branch | Function |
|---|---|
| **Executive** | Day-to-day capital allocation, operations |
| **Legislative** | Charter amendments, policy, rules of the collective |
| **Judicial** | Dispute resolution via **AI + law + sortition** (random jury-style voting — ancient Greek/Atheinian model) |

### Founder Veto
During the founder's life (or until a solid constitutional structure is in place), the primary founder retains **veto power over all decisions**.

### Constitutional Sunset
The founder's authority sunsets over time into:
- A constitutionally-encoded governance mandate
- An **endowment model with external managers** (portfolio of capital allocators under contract)
- A charter protection entity whose entire job is to protect the PBC mission

### Stewardship-Based Governance (Not Democracy)

> The spirit is **stewardship over your plot or responsibility**. The goal is to create and help people become better and better stewards, taking on more and more responsibility.

Not "one worker, one vote." A top-down structure is fine **as long as the charter protects against any one person taking over** (beyond the founder's initial veto).

---

## 4. The Stewardship Ladder

Knowledge, strategy, and understanding are given **line upon line** as people prove their stewardship. Progression can happen at different speeds, but gateways must be hit. Long-term stewardship and reputation are tracked and grown.

| Level | Rights & Access | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **Member** | Base annuity, capital account starts compounding | Do their job well |
| **Senior Member** | Borrow up to 25% of account | Mentors others |
| **Steward** | Borrow up to 50%, access to internal investments | Allocates capital within their domain, responsible for a P&L |
| **Capital Steward** | Borrow up to 75%, entity-level allocation decisions | Helps set investment strategy |
| **Board / Constitutional Council** | Full access, charter-bound | Protects the mission, selects managers |

**Key principle**: Mistakes are encouraged — learn and grow. But rights, freedoms, and privileges are **based on current and continued stewardship over time**.

## 5. Capital Account Structure (The Core Innovation)

### Individual LLC Model
Each individual (and each business) has **their own LLC** (possibly Series LLCs). They license their labor, ideas, IP to the PBC. This creates:

- **Legal separation** for each capital account
- **IP ownership stays with creators** — they control and own their IP, The Collectiv licenses it from them
- **Banking on the creator economy** — projected $250B → $1.3T by 2033

### The Annuity + Progressive Access Model

> **A lifetime annuity funded by a compounding internal capital account, where the principal is permanent (stays with the collective), but borrowing capacity against it grows progressively from youth through maturity.**

| Life Stage | Access |
|---|---|
| **Birth → 18** | Account compounds, no access |
| **~18** | Small borrowing capacity unlocked (~10%) |
| **~25** | Moderate borrowing capacity (~25%) |
| **~30** | Significant borrowing capacity (~50%) |
| **~40+** | Full borrowing capacity |
| **Retirement → Death** | Lifetime annuity payments based on account balance |

**Design decisions locked in:**
- No cash-out ever — the annuity IS the benefit, not the principal
- When a member dies, the account stays with the collective
- Kids build from a young age — no windfall, just expanding capability
- The marginal cost of adding a member = annuity payment, not the capital account value

### Collaborative Attribution System

> With AI, crypto, ledgers, and machine learning, for the first time we can track who did what to contribute to the overall whole.

- Net new work product attributed to multiple collaborators
- Reward is based on **contribution to the whole**
- If someone's work inspires or unlocks something new, original creators get credit
- **The Collectiv as a whole owns end products, data, and net new IP**
- Creators get **rights to the upside** of what they create
- If someone doesn't like not owning their own thing, they're welcome to build elsewhere

## 6. Capital Sources (Phase 1)

### Three Initial Funding Streams

1. **AI Implementation Services Business**
   - Licensing model: individuals pay for a license to use The Collectiv's stack
   - Setup and run their own individual AI businesses
   - Recurring revenue from the license pool

2. **External Investors**
   - **Non-accredited**: Reg CF — up to $5M/year crowdfund cap (or spin up separate LLCs each with their own $5M cap)
   - **Accredited**: Traditional private placement (Reg D 506(c))
   - Self-directed IRAs (via Alto, iTrustCapital, Rocket Dollar, Kingdom Trust)
   - Annual independent 409A valuation

3. **Cooperative Membership Fees**
   - Similar to Mondragón's ~11,000€ fee deducted from future wages
   - Or financed via Affirm loan or similar
   - One-time membership gives access to the ecosystem

### Target Capital Sources (Mature Entity)

| Source | Structure |
|---|---|
| Family offices | Non-voting common + borrowing facility, buy-borrow-die |
| Sovereign wealth funds | Large non-voting stakes, seat on investment committee |
| Pension funds / 401ks | Fund vehicle or structured note wrapper |
| Insurance / endowments | Preferred shares with cumulative dividend |

## 7. Asset Strategy

### 250-Year AI-Resilient Assets

| Asset Class | Durability | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Land / real location assets | Very high | Data center land, ports, last-mile fiber rights, energy transmission |
| Energy infrastructure | High | Solar leases, wind royalties, transmission rights |
| Regulated utilities | High | Government-backed returns, natural monopolies |
| Data center / compute infrastructure | Medium | Own the real estate and power, not the compute |
| Insurance / reinsurance | Medium-high | Fundamental risk pooling |
| IP / royalty portfolios | Medium-low | Active management needed |
| Operating businesses | Low | Rotate capital through them |

### The Berkshire Model Applied
You don't build businesses that last 250 years. You build a **capital allocation machine** that owns and rotates through businesses over the centuries.

## 8. Buy-Borrow-Die Mechanics

- **Margin lending partner** (eventually building own lending facility)
- Shares accepted as collateral against NAV
- Borrow against growing principle for liquidity needs
- **Step-up in basis** at death (current US tax law — track this)
- The entity is designed so that selling shares is never the optimal move — borrowing is

## 9. The "Becoming the Destination" Dynamic

As the entity grows, its own capital base becomes the largest source of returns. At scale, the question flips from "where do we find good investments?" to "how do we deploy capital fast enough at our required returns?"

**Positioning for this:**
- High trust, steady consistency from Day 1
- Show a 10+ year track record before going for the largest institutional pools
- Build permanent, capital-hungry asset pipelines early (infrastructure, energy, private credit)
